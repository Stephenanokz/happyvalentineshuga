The Early Days

Comfort. Curiosity. Connection.

The early days felt light.

Everything was new, effortless, and full of curiosity.
We talked without forcing it.
We laughed without thinking.
We stayed up late with each other.
There was comfort in the way things unfolded naturally,
like we didn’t need to try too hard to be understood.

It was simple. It was honest. And somehow, it already felt familiar
like something meaningful was quietly taking shape.

Falling In Love

Love didn’t rush, it settled.

The Joy

The moments that felt like home.

The joy came in moments both big and small.

In shared laughter.
In pictures we didn’t plan to take.
In memories that still warm my heart when I think about them.

It was the kind of joy that felt safe. The kind that made ordinary days
feel special. The kind that reminded me how beautiful love can be when
it’s genuine, present, and shared.

The Hard Parts

What We Learned

Love is a choice, and we choose it.

We learned that love isn’t just about the good moments.

It’s about patience.
It’s about listening.
It’s about choosing understanding even when it’s difficult.

We learned that growth can be uncomfortable,
but it’s also necessary.
And that loving someone means learning how to grow
both together and individually
without losing sight of what matters.
